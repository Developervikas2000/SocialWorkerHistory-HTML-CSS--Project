Notable Indian Social Workers

India has a rich history of social work, with many individuals dedicating their lives to improving the welfare of others. Here are some notable Indian social workers:

  • Mother Teresa

    Mother Teresa, also known as Saint Teresa of Calcutta, was a Roman Catholic nun and missionary. She founded the Missionaries of Charity, a Roman Catholic religious congregation, which manages homes for people dying of HIV/AIDS, leprosy, and tuberculosis. She received the Nobel Peace Prize in 1979.

  • Mahatma Gandhi

    Mohandas Karamchand Gandhi, also known as Mahatma Gandhi, was a leader of the Indian independence movement and a staunch advocate of non-violence and civil rights. He worked tirelessly for the upliftment of the underprivileged and promoted social reforms.

  • Medha Patkar

    Medha Patkar is a social activist known for her role in the Narmada Bachao Andolan, a movement to save the Narmada River and the people who depend on it from the impacts of large dam projects. She has worked extensively on various social issues, including housing rights and women's rights.

  • Ela Bhatt

    Ela Bhatt is the founder of the Self-Employed Women's Association of India (SEWA), a trade union for poor, self-employed women workers. She has been a strong advocate for women's rights and has worked towards the economic empowerment of women in India.

  • Aruna Roy

    Aruna Roy is a social activist known for her efforts in the Right to Information movement in India. She co-founded the Mazdoor Kisan Shakti Sangathan (MKSS), a grassroots organization focused on empowering workers and farmers.
